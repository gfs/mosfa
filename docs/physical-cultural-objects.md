# Physical Cultural Objects Accession Program

## Curatorial Thesis

The Pearl Mask of the Salt King is compelling because it behaves like a real object from a real civilization: it has material logic, ritual function, body-specific form, and implied systems around it. The next physical accessions should fortify that same credibility by widening the ancient world beyond rulers and temples into civic power, family life, medicine, labor, music, and archives.

## Priority Candidates

### Crown of the Brine Regent

The strongest companion to the Pearl Mask. Where the mask is funerary and inward, the crown is public and civic: an object engineered for antennae, crest, and court visibility. It should feel weighty, fitted, slightly impractical, and too solemn to be decorative.

Generation direction: isolated museum object photography, shell-gold crown, pearl nodes, antenna-like arcs, coral prongs, mineral salt, hinge details, dark neutral plinth, ancient reef-regalia craftsmanship.

### Molting Ledger of the Seven Currents

The best worldbuilding candidate after the crown because it makes shrimp-folk biology part of bureaucracy and lineage. The object should read as a shell book or archival reliquary, with translucent molt membranes mounted like pages, pearl seals, and tide glyph indexing.

Generation direction: hinged shell ledger open on archival support, translucent exuviae sheets, tide glyphs, pearl seals, mineral staining, careful archaeological lighting.

### Divers' Oath Standard

This object connects royal pearl culture to labor. It gives the pearl economy a guild structure, oath culture, and maritime danger. The standard should not look like a human flag pasted underwater; it needs weight, current-resistant design, knot logic, and carved claw emblems.

Generation direction: coral pole with weighted base, kelp-fiber banner, pearl-knot cords, carved guild marks, worn grip zones, storm-darkened edges, object study against a museum wall.

### Cradle Shell of the Moon Pool

This is the intimate counterweight to dynastic material. It should make domestic life visible without becoming cute: protective, handled, slightly sacred, and small enough to imply touch.

Generation direction: polished cradle shell, moon-pool glyphs, pearl rattles, coral protective teeth, softened edges from handling, warm museum case lighting.

### Tide Physician's Pearl Cupping Kit

This adds knowledge work and care. The object should sit between medicine and ritual: specialized instruments, fitted compartments, diagnostic cords, tiny cups, and material evidence of use.

Generation direction: mother-of-pearl case with compartments, shell cups, coral lancets, tide-mark cords, mineral stains, precise tool arrangement, archaeological catalogue photography.

### Current Harp of the Lower Reef

This expands the civilization into sound. The harp should be beautiful, but not decorative fantasy: it needs a credible acoustic premise in water currents, shell resonance, and temple procession.

Generation direction: shell harp with coral ribs, pearl tuning beads, glyph-incised base, current channels, polished wear at contact points, solemn temple-instrument presence.

## Accession Strategy

Generate the Crown first because it can immediately converse with the Pearl Mask as a pair of dynastic icons. Follow with the Molting Ledger or Divers' Oath Standard to prove the culture is not only royal, then add the Cradle Shell to bring emotional scale into the wing.
