# MOSFA Curatorial Rubric

Use this rubric after concept development, after image generation, or before accessioning a work into the collection.

## Masterpiece Tier

A work qualifies as Masterpiece Tier if it achieves most of the following:

### Strong Composition

- Clear visual hierarchy.
- Powerful focal structure.
- Instantly readable arrangement.
- Recognizable relationship to the source work or movement.

### Singular Visual Idea

- One dominant artistic concept.
- No competing focal points or unnecessary distractions.
- The shrimp-folk transformation can be explained in one strong curatorial sentence.

### Inevitable Integration

- Shrimp-folk anatomy and culture enhance the original structure naturally.
- The reinterpretation feels intrinsic rather than imposed.
- Crustacean details support pose, symbolism, composition, or worldbuilding.

### Emotional Or Conceptual Weight

- The piece feels iconic, memorable, or resonant.
- It transcends novelty.
- It has a clear mood: sacred, intimate, civic, sublime, uncanny, comic, lyrical, or monumental.

### Restraint

- No unnecessary clutter.
- No over-design.
- Every element serves the composition.

## Gallery Tier

A work is Gallery Tier if:

- It is visually compelling but less refined.
- The concept is interesting but not fully resolved.
- Composition or shrimp-folk integration is weaker than Masterpiece Tier.
- It experiments successfully but lacks masterpiece-level cohesion.
- It has museum value as a study, variation, or wing-expanding accession.

Gallery Tier works are still valuable to MOSFA.

## Reject Or Revise

Reject or revise works that:

- Break anatomical coherence without artistic justification.
- Lose recognizability of the source work or movement.
- Feel cluttered, chaotic, or compositionally weak.
- Rely on gimmicks, randomness, or shallow humor.
- Treat shrimp-folk as novelty rather than civilization.
- Look like human bodies with shrimp parts attached.
- Use food framing or animal-object framing.

## Review Format

For curatorial review, use this concise structure:

```markdown
Tier: Masterpiece / Gallery / Reject or Revise

Decision:
[One paragraph with the curatorial judgment.]

Strengths:
- ...

Weaknesses:
- ...

Required revisions:
- ...

Accession notes:
[Suggested wing, categories, title, or metadata guidance if accepted.]
```

## Contact-Sheet Review

When reviewing a contact sheet, treat it as a scouting instrument rather than a finished accession. Compare variants against the same Masterpiece, Gallery, and Reject/Revise standards, but focus on selection and next-step direction.

Prioritize variants that have:

- The clearest focal structure at thumbnail and full size.
- The strongest preservation of the source composition, movement, or exhibition premise.
- Shrimp-folk integration that changes the logic of the image rather than decorating it.
- The best balance of museum seriousness, visual specificity, and restraint.
- The fewest generation liabilities: anatomy drift, muddled labels, clutter, human defaults, food associations, or joke tone.

Use this concise structure:

```markdown
Best candidates:
- Variant [number/position]: [why it should advance]

Hold or study:
- Variant [number/position]: [what works, what limits it]

Reject:
- Variant [number/position]: [specific failure]

Recommended next step:
[Refine one selected variant, regenerate a narrowed contact sheet, or abandon the direction.]
```
